1. The short version

Jit has no server and no database. Everything you type — your income, filing status, questions — is processed entirely inside your own browser and is never transmitted to us, because there is no "us" on the other end of a request. There is nothing for us to collect, store, sell, or lose in a breach, because we never receive it in the first place.

2. What's stored, and where

Jit uses your browser's local storage (localStorage) to remember things between visits, entirely on your device:

  • Income, filing status, and state you've mentioned in chat, so you don't have to repeat yourself
  • Display preferences (compact layout, etc.)
  • An optional Google Gemini API key, if you choose to enter one for AI-assist
  • An optional Jit Pro license key, if you purchase and activate Jit Pro

None of this is sent to any server we operate. It stays on your device until you clear it (via "Clear conversation," clearing the Pro key, or clearing your browser's site data) or your browser clears it itself.

3. Third parties you opt into directly

Two features call third-party services directly from your browser — never routed through any server of ours:

  • AI assist (optional, off by default): if you enter your own Google Gemini API key, unmatched questions are sent directly to Google's Gemini API from your browser. Google's own privacy policy governs that exchange, not this one.
  • Jit Pro license verification (only if you purchase Pro): your license key is sent directly to the payment processor's (Gumroad's) license-verification API from your browser to confirm it's valid. Gumroad's own privacy policy governs that exchange, and whatever information you provided Gumroad during purchase (e.g., email address) is held by Gumroad, not by us.

4. Cookies and tracking

Jit does not use cookies, analytics, or advertising trackers of any kind. The only client-side storage is the localStorage described above.

5. Your choices

Because everything lives in your browser, you're always in full control: clear it via the in-app "Clear conversation" button, the Pro settings' "Deactivate" button, or your browser's own site-data controls (Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data, or equivalent).
